I cheated on this one a little, the others let me pick to cook here. I'd spotted them in the list and researched them in advance because I prefer the challenge of finding recipes from less well known places. When I say less well known, maybe that should only apply to me as my mum knew exactly where these islands are.

Turks and Caicos islands have more than a passing mention in history. They were 'discovered' in 1492 by Christopher Columbus, John Glenn touched down just off the coast in 1962 and a proposed union is Canada is on the cards.

The best bit is that I got to cook something I've wanted to try for ages - Saltfish and Ackee! I came across a mention of ackee as a child, I can't remember where probably a natural history program, and it stuck in my mind because it can be poisonous ^_^



Only the flesh of ackee is safe to eat, and then only when it has naturally opened, otherwise you can suffer from Jamaican vomiting sickness.

Saltfish and Ackee based on this recipe
4 portions of cod
1 tin of ackee (the tins stocked here are oversized, 1 1/2 times the size of standard tins)
1 onion, thinly sliced
1 red pepper, thinly sliced
1/2 scotch bonnet chili
1 tsp black pepper
Oil

Fry the onion and pepper until soft, set aside half. Add the cod, ackee pepper and chili. Cook for 15 / 20 minutes stirring occasionally. Serve and garnish with the onion and pepper set aside earlier.


Rice and Peas Based on this recipe
1 onion, sliced
1 red pepper, sliced
6 rashers of bacon, sliced
2 tbs tomato paste
2 tbs olive oil
1 can pigeon peas (sometimes called Gungo pea)
50g brown rice
150 / 200ml water
1 vegetable stock cube
salt
fresh thyme
bay leaf
black pepper
oil

Fry the onion, pepper and bacon until the onion goes soft / bacon cooked. Add the tomato paste, peas, salt, thyme, bay leaf, black pepper, water, stock cube and rice. Simmer until the rice is cooked.

Both these recipes are simple, really quick and so tasty! Whilst the ackee / gungo peas are speciality ingredients around here, I expect black-eyed or pinto beans could be substituted in the rice and peas. That recipe is going straight into my stock when I have surprise guests.
