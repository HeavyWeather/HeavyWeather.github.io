--- layout: post title: Finland ---



First of all Finnish food is unusual. Really rather unusual, probably the most unusual food I've ever cooked. Primarily the recipes seem to be meat and root vegetables. The two most popular recipes I found were for Karelian Stew and Janssons Frestelse. I was put off by the various recipes I read for Karelian Stew online - the shear quantity of meat especially the quantity of offal in quite a few of the versions.... urrgh!

So, the meal I cooked was Janssons Frestelse (Jansson's Temptation) an interesting potato and anchovy gratin. It really wasn't my first choice, but I was determined to cook something different and wouldn't consider cooking with offal. It was actually surprisingly nice - and this is coming from someone who doesn't like seafood. I served it with Karelian Potato pasties. Unlike Cornish pasties these pasties are open, as it says in the recipe think of them as little pasty boats ^_^ Also Lanttulaatikko (Rutabaga Casserole). Rutabaga is a swede. This is a wonderful recipe, its so simple and tasty. With the addition of maybe a little mixed spice you have a very interesting and christmasy side dish.

I haven't posted any recipes this time because I cooked everything without making any amendments.
